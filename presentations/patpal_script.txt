Petpal!

PetPal is a mobile and web application for a chain of veterinary clinics across the Scottish Highlands. The application allows pet owners to book appointments, view their pet's vaccination and treatment history, receive automated reminders, and pay invoices online. Vets can access patient records, add treatment notes, and generate prescriptions.

In order to deliver this within the alloted time, effective resource management is key. 

Each sprint planning will define specific and attainable goals to be completed within the sprints duration.

To determine the most effective development process for this project, we will compare Scrum with Extreme Programming (or XP).

Scrum focuses on structured, time-boxed sprints, clear roles such as the product owner and scrum master, and regular feedback through sprint reviews. This makes it highly effective for maintaining organisation, especially when working with a non-technical client, as it provides predictable delivery and frequent opportunities for feedback.

In contrast, XP places a stronger emphasis on engineering practices. These include pair programming, continuous integration, and test-driven development. XP is particularly focused on code quality and rapid adaptability to changing requirements.

[NEXT SLIDE]

Given our team structure — a small, co-located team of five developers — XP offers some clear advantages.

For example, pair programming could significantly improve code quality and knowledge sharing within the team. Since we are working closely together, this would be practical to implement and would reduce the risk of bugs early in development.

However, XP lacks the strong project management structure that Scrum provides. Considering our client is non-technical, having clearly defined roles, sprint reviews, and a single point of communication is essential. Scrum handles this far better than XP.

[NEXT SLIDE]

Because of this, the recommended approach is to primarily use Scrum, while incorporating selected XP practices like pair programming when dealing with difficult problems.

This hybrid approach allows us to maintain strong communication, planning, and stakeholder involvement through Scrum, while improving technical quality through XP practices such as pair programming and continuous testing.

While onboarding a dedicated QA specialist increases the budget, and the existing developers may already be well equipped to test their own work, doing so carries additional benefits that are worth considering.

[NEXT SLIDE]
Hiring a dedicated QA specialist brings independent unbiased oversight to the project, which is critical for ensuring reliability in a healthcare-related system like PetPal.

While developers already test their own work, they are naturally more focused on building new features. This can create blind spots within certain edge cases, or integrations - Something a feature-building-oriented mind might miss, but a user and risk oriented mind might not.

[NEXT SLIDE]
For PetPal specifically, this matters because the system handles sensitive and time-critical tasks such as appointment bookings, medical records, and prescription generation. This means that if a developer-made error goes unnoticed, the consequences could be quite severe; Incorrect or duplicate billing, missed appointments, or incorrect treatment. All of which would directly impact the clinic's reputation and the quality of treatment.


[NEXT SLIDE]
Another key advantage is consistency and accountability. 

With a dedicated QA role, quality becomes a job description (or a defined responsibility) rather than a shared assumption. The QA lead can establish more rigorous testing standards or acceptance criteria, ensuring that every feature meets a consistent level of quality before release.

This is especially important in an Agile Scrum environment, where frequent releases can otherwise lead to quality slipping over time.

[NEXT SLIDE]

Finally, investing in a QA lead is more cost-effective in the long run.

Fixing bugs after deployment is significantly more expensive than catching them early. More importantly, failures in a veterinary system can damage client trust, which is far harder to recover than the cost of prevention.

Ensuring security and reliability of the service regardless of internet connectivity is of critical importance. Given the healthcare-based nature of this project, and the fact that the application handles sensitive and health-critical information, as well as processes payments, appropriate security measures will be taken,

[NEXT SLIDE]
To ensure that the service runs without interruptions in areas with poor connectivity,
processes will be handled internally on the client device and only the results of those processes will then be transmitted to the server on the nearest network availability.

To prevent data loss during any process (lengthy or short), progress will be saved on the client's device locally.

[NEXT SLIDE]
Both the web app as well as the mobile app will commit progress to local memory with every change. In case of the web app, localStorage may be used as it allows sufficient storage for the operational needs.
[NEXT SLIDE]

All communications between the client and the server will be conducted via HTTPS - This is a much more robust protocol that vastly mitigates our exposure to man-in-the-middle attacks.

[NEXT SLIDE]
For payment transactions, we are lawfully required to use PCI-DSS, and for a good reason. 
PCI-DSS stands for Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard, and it is a globally recognised standard for processing payment transactions, wherein all payment transactions are done through a reputable mediator such as Visa or MasterCard - We don't get any of the details, just the result of the transactions.
